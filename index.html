To Read Main Log Favorites

Letters to Milena

Franz Kafka and Milena Jesenka

My favorite genre of book is collections of real letters between real people. These letters are particularly interesting because thier affair had been entirely within these letters. Their very contrasting lives and identities kind of make this a sad but interesting read. I do wish there was more letters available from Milena's side.

We Need to Talk About Kevin

Lionel Shriver

Probably my favorite book when it comes to the nature vs. nurture theme. Follows the letters of a mother of a school shooter.

Braiding Sweetgrass

Robin Wall Kimmerer

Although I think some of the points are debatable, the writing style is beautifully poetic.

The Stolen White Elephant

Mark Twain

The only Mark Twain I've read other than parts of his autobiography. I didn't expect it to be so absurd, but this was really funny!

What If Women Designed the City?

May East

Reads like a casual textbook, but gives some really interesting concepts and ideas that make cities more sustainable and friendly.

The Future is Analog: How to Create a More Human World

David Sax

Although I don't agree with every single point the author makes, I think the book makes a really important commentary on the importance of quality physical time with one another,

The Social Contract

Rousseau

I've read excerpts of this book many times, so I thought I'd go ahead and read the whole thing. As expected some of the ideas (especially in the latter half) are outdated or state some false facts but overall really interesting.

Anarchism and Other Essays

Emma Goldman

Emma Goldman quickly became one of my favorite writers. Except for maybe one or two essays on the events of her time, all the essays here were really interesting to think about and read.

How to Blow Up A Pipeline

Andreas Malm

Athough the book reads more like one big thesis, its very good at making its point. Theres a movie based on this book and I'd want to watch it.

How to Live Safely in a Science Fictional Universe

Charles Yu

I love metafiction and this book was super fun to read. It was a bit emotional at times.

Giovanni's Room

James Baldwin

One of my favorites! I think its really interesting how despite this book being written by James Baldwin, it takes place in Europe and is absent of any characters of color. Its themes mostly focus on queerness and identity.

I, Who Has Never Known Men

Jacqueline Harpman

Short, but I think the point the book makes can apply outside of the female context the book uses. Really cool feminist food for thought.

Forgiving Imelda Marcos

Nathan Go

I think it was kinda of weird to use a real-life politican for the subject of the book, but I loved the importance of the cultural/historical context when it came to the relationships between characters.

The Diary of Adam and Eve

Mark Twain

This was a really funny short read. The title is very literal.

A Thousand Splendid Suns

Khaled Hosseini

Really sad, but also really good.

The Testaments

Margret Atwood

Although I think as a sequel to the Handmaids Tale the plot was very influenced by the show and other adaptations, the importance and meaning of the story are still the same. Especially now more than ever, eveyrone should read the Handmaids Tale and understand where it comes from.

All Art is Propaganda

George Orwell

Normally I like Orwell, but these essays were kinda boring.

The Athiests Mass

Honore de Balzac

A good short story, gives a more nuanced view on having a relationship with spirituality and religon.

The Ones Who Stay and Fight

N.K Jemisen

I think 'Those Who Walk Away from Omelas' is a really compelling story, but I think this response to it a much more nuanced and optimistic take on that moral dilemma.

The Yellow Wallpaper

Charlotte Perkins Gilman

Although it was a short story, the progression was very intense. Although it isn't a horror/thriller, it definitely kept your attention in a similar way.
